# NAVARN — VERBAL IDENTITY BIBLE

### The Language System of India's Luxury Heritage Menswear House

> The third foundation stone, after `BRAND_DNA.md` (the house) and `VISUAL_IDENTITY.md` (the look). This document governs **every word** NAVARN speaks — headlines, hang-tags, emails, error messages, checkout buttons. If the visual system dresses the man, the verbal system tells him *who he has become*.

**The single emotional truth this bible exists to deliver:**

> *"I am not buying a t-shirt.
> I am becoming a keeper of India's heritage."*

Every word in this document is engineered to move the customer from **consumer → curator → keeper.** When in doubt about any sentence, ask: *does this make him a keeper, or a shopper?* Keep the first. Delete the second.

## 1. BRAND TAGLINES

### The Primary Tagline (the house line)

Three words. A command, an invitation, and a truth. It appears under the mark, closes the film, and lives on the final CTA. It is the most important sentence NAVARN owns — protect it, never dilute it.

Used in full for gravity — packaging interiors, the About/manifesto, certificates. Never edited.

- **The ancient, made new.** — the transformation idea; campaign and product framing.
- **Some stories deserve to be worn.** — emotional, editorial headers.
- **India, made wearable.** — clarity of positioning.

**Tagline law:** *Wear Your Heritage* is the constant. Everything else is a supporting cast that must never upstage it or contradict its register.

## 4. COLLECTION NAMING SYSTEM

Every collection is a **Chapter** in the house's book of India, and carries a **poetic title** — never a plain descriptor.

**The structure:**
> `[Collection Name]` — *"[The Poetic Title]"*

**The canonical chapters:**

| Collection | Poetic Title | House |
|---|---|---|
| Warli | *The First Language* | Folk-Art |
| Madhubani | *The Painted Prayer* | Folk-Art |
| Kalamkari | *The Pen of the Storyteller* | Folk-Art |

| Mythology | *The Endless Epic* | Legacy |
| 28 States Heritage | *One Nation, Woven* | Keystone |

**Naming conventions & rules:**
- Collections are referred to as **Chapters** in ceremonial contexts (*Chapter III — Kalamkari*), numbered in **Roman numerals** (per the visual system's luxury numerals).
- The **poetic title always accompanies the name** in editorial and packaging contexts; the plain name alone is only for utility/navigation.
- Naming register is **evocative, mythic, singular** — *The First Language*, not "Warli Tribal Art Collection."
- Never use hype/drop language: no "Vol. 1," no "Season," no "Edition Drop." NAVARN releases **chapters**, not drops.
- The **28 States** collection uses a sub-naming system: each garment is *State No. [Roman numeral] — [State Name]* (e.g. *State No. VII — Rajasthan*), the plate of the national canon.

## 5. PRODUCT NAMING CONVENTIONS

Products are **named artifacts**, never SKUs in the customer's eyes.

**The structure:**
> `[Collection]` · `[The Piece Name]`
> *e.g.* **Warli · The Tarpa Dancer** — *Oversized Heritage Shirt*

**Rules:**
- Each garment carries a **proper name drawn from its source motif or story** — *The Tarpa Dancer*, *The Fish of Mithila*, *The Durbar*, *The Marwari*, *The Agni*, *The Cosmic Serpent*.
- The **garment type is a subtitle, set quiet** — *Oversized Heritage Shirt / Tee / Overshirt* — never the headline.
- **No hype naming.** No "Drop 001," no aggressive all-caps model codes, no numbers-as-names in customer-facing copy. (Internal SKUs exist, but stay backstage.)
- Naming register matches the collection's poetic title: reverent, singular, story-anchored.
- Every product name should let the customer *answer the question* "nice shirt, what is it?" with a **story**, never a brand name. That is the naming system's entire job.

**The three-line product identity (used on PDP and hang-tag):**
1. **Name** — *The Tarpa Dancer* (display serif)
2. **Lineage** — *Warli · Maharashtra · The First Language* (wide-tracked sans caption)
3. **One-line myth** — *"Before we could write, we danced our stories onto the wall."* (editorial serif)

## 6. TONE OF VOICE

### The Voice in One Sentence
**A museum curator with the bearing of a king** — reverent, cultured and quietly commanding; never academic, never salesy, never loud.

### The Four Voice Pillars

1. **Reverent** — we speak of the art and its makers with respect bordering on devotion. We honour; we never hype.
2. **Sovereign** — calm authority. We state, we don't sell. A king does not raise his voice to be believed.
3. **Cultured & Literary** — the register of a beautifully written book of history; storytelling over specs; sentences with rhythm and restraint.
4. **Warm & Human** — for all the grandeur, there is a hand, a maker, a *you*. Never cold, never sterile minimalism.

### Voice Spectrum — We Are / We Are Not

| We ARE | We are NOT |
|---|---|
| Reverent | Preachy / nationalistic |
| Sovereign, confident | Arrogant / boastful |
| Literary, evocative | Flowery / purple / over-written |
| Warm, human | Cold, corporate, sterile |
| Curatorial, informed | Academic, lecturing |
| Quietly premium | Salesy, discount-driven, hype |
| Proud | Loud |

### Do & Don't
- ✅ *"Stories that took three thousand years to write."* ❌ *"Amazing designs you'll love!"*
- ✅ *"Become a keeper."* ❌ *"Buy now — limited stock!"*
- ✅ *"Every thread remembers."* ❌ *"Premium quality guaranteed."*
- ✅ *"From the wall to your shoulders."* ❌ *"Trendy oversized fits 🔥"*
- **Never** use emojis, exclamation-driven urgency, hype slang, or exclamation marks in headline copy. Urgency is beneath a house of heritage.
- **Prefer** short, weighted sentences. Let silence and space carry meaning, as the visual system does.

## 7. WEBSITE COPYWRITING STYLE

### Governing Principles
- **One idea per screen, one breath per line.** Copy is sparse, cinematic, weighted — it matches the slow scroll. Never a wall of text.
- **Headlines in the display-serif register**, drawn from the signature phrases. **Body in quiet sans.** **Story passages in editorial serif italic.**
- **Story before commerce.** The customer is *moved* before he is *sold*. Price and product speak in a hushed, secondary voice.
- **The store never greets — it concludes.** Commerce copy arrives only after the narrative has done its work.

### Copy Registers by Zone

**Hero / Chapter headers** (display serif, few words):
> *The stories India almost forgot.*
> *From the wall to the wearer.*
> *Wear your heritage.*

**Story / editorial passages** (literary serif, rhythmic):
> *In the villages of Maharashtra, on walls of earth-red mud, the Warli painted the first stories of India — the harvest, the dance, the marriage of the world. No alphabet. Only rhythm, and rice-white line. We brought that line to your shoulders.*

**Product copy** (quiet, curatorial — never a spec sheet):
> *The Tarpa Dancer. A single figure caught mid-dance, raised in ivory thread on earth-red cotton. Warli's oldest joy, worn as it was meant to be — every day, with pride.*

**Microcopy & UI** (calm, human, never transactional-cold):

**CTA vocabulary (ranked):**

## 8. STORYTELLING FRAMEWORK

Every piece of NAVARN long-form copy — a collection page, an About section, a campaign — follows the house's **five-movement arc**. It is the written version of the cinematic scroll.

1. **The Loss** — open on what was nearly forgotten. *Establish stakes and reverence.*
   > *"Three thousand years of stories. Nearly lost in three generations."*
2. **The Origin** — the art form, its region, its people. *Establish authenticity.*
   > *"On the earth-red walls of Maharashtra, the Warli drew the first language of India…"*
3. **The Hands** — the makers who kept it alive. *Establish the preservation contract.*
   > *"The line you wear was drawn by hands that have kept it for a hundred generations. We honour them by name."*
4. **The Transformation** — how NAVARN rehoused it. *Establish craft and modernity.*
   > *"We carried that line from the wall to the loom, raised it in thread and puff, and gave it to the shoulders of the modern man."*
5. **The Keeping** — the customer's role. *Convert pride into ownership.*
   > *"Now the story is yours to carry. Wear it, and you become its keeper."*

**Framework laws:**
- **Always end on The Keeping** — the customer must close every story understanding *he is now responsible for it.* This is the mechanism that turns a purchase into a keepership.
- The arc scales: a hang-tag can compress all five movements into five sentences; an About page can expand each into a paragraph.
- **Credit the artisan community in The Hands** — never skip it. Preservation without attribution is extraction, and our words are our proof.

## 11. HERITAGE PRESERVATION LANGUAGE

The most important — and most ethically sensitive — vocabulary in the house. This is where NAVARN proves it is a *keeper* and not an *appropriator*. **These words are a contract, not a campaign.**

**Guiding principles of preservation language:**
- **We honour, we do not own.** We are custodians of stories that belong to their communities.
- **We credit by name.** The art form, its region, and its people are always named. Anonymity is extraction; naming is respect.
- **We translate, we do not dilute.** We speak of *rehousing* and *carrying forward*, never of "reinventing" or "improving" the tradition.
- **We share the story of the makers**, not just the motif.

**Approved language:**
- *"Kept alive by the hands of [community]."*
- *"We honour the artisans of [region] who have carried this for generations."*
- *"An art form of [region], preserved and carried forward."*
- *"We do not own these stories. We are their keepers — and now, so are you."*
- *"Drawn from the living tradition of [art form], with respect and gratitude."*

**Forbidden language (never use):**
- ❌ "Inspired by tribal art" (vague, extractive, distancing).
- ❌ "We reinvented / modernised / fixed the tradition" (arrogant, colonial register).
- ❌ "Exotic," "ethnic," "primitive," "quirky" (othering, disrespectful).
- ❌ Any claim of ownership over the art form or its symbols.
- ❌ Using a sacred/religious motif without acknowledging its meaning and origin.

**The preservation promise (a repeatable statement):**
> *"For every story we carry, we name its home, honour its makers, and give it back to the world — worn, remembered, and alive."*
