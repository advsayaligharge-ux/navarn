# NAVARN — BRAND DNA

### The Luxury Heritage Menswear House of India

> *"To preserve India's stories through premium fashion."*

This document is the constitution of NAVARN. Every garment, photograph, word, pixel and gesture that carries the name must be able to trace itself back to a line written here. Read it before you design anything.

---

## 0. The One Truth

**NAVARN turns India's civilizational memory into wearable heritage.**

We do not print Indian motifs on t-shirts. We take a living art form — a Warli wall, a Madhubani fish, a Maharaja's court — and we *rehouse* it in modern, oversized, luxury-finished garments so a man can wear his civilization every day, not once at a wedding.

The garment is the medium. The culture is the product. Never invert this.

# 1. BRAND MANIFESTO

### Who We Are

We are NAVARN — a luxury heritage menswear house.

We are custodians before we are a clothing label. We are the keepers of stories that took three thousand years to write and were nearly forgotten in three generations. We are the bridge between the artisan's wall and the modern man's shoulders.

We are not streetwear. We are not fast fashion. We are not "ethnic wear." We are the first house built to make Indian heritage a daily luxury — not a costume, not an occasion, but a way of being dressed.

*Nav* — new. NAVARN is the ancient, made new.

### Why We Exist

Because a civilization that produced the Warli, the Gond, the Kalamkari, the courts of the Maharajas and the epics of gods — the richest visual and narrative heritage on Earth — watched its young men grow up dressing in someone else's logos, someone else's stories, someone else's idea of luxury.

We exist to end that quietly, and beautifully.

We exist because pride should be wearable. Because an artisan's line should outlive the wall it was painted on. Because "made in India" should mean *made of India* — and should command a premium, not apologize for a price.

We exist to preserve India's stories through premium fashion. That is not a tagline. It is the reason the company is allowed to take up space in the world.

### What We Stand For

- **Reverence over appropriation.** We honor the art and its origin community. We elevate and credit; we never extract and forget.
- **Craft over hype.** The premium is earned in the hand — the weight of the fabric, the density of the embroidery, the rise of the puff — not claimed by a price tag.
- **Story over decoration.** If a garment cannot tell you why it exists, it does not ship.
- **Modernity over nostalgia.** We are not a museum. We are heritage that lives on a moving body, in a moving city, today.
- **Pride over trend.** We do not chase the season. We build the canon.

We stand for the Indian man who no longer wants to choose between his roots and his style — because we refuse to let those be two different things.

# 2. BRAND PHILOSOPHY

NAVARN rests on three pillars. A garment is only NAVARN if it stands on all three.

### I. Heritage Preservation

Every collection begins in the field, not the studio. We source from the living tradition — the art form, the region, the community that carries it. Preservation is not a marketing claim for us; it is a supply chain and a moral contract:

- We work *with* artisan clusters and origin communities, and we attribute them.
- We treat the source art form as sacred reference, not raw material — we translate it, we never dilute it.
- A portion of every collection's story is the story of the people who kept it alive.

Preservation is the difference between a brand that *takes* from India and a house that *keeps* it. We are a keeper.

### II. Modern Luxury

Heritage without luxury is a handicraft stall. Luxury without heritage is a foreign logo. NAVARN is the rare intersection.

- **Silhouette:** modern, oversized, architectural — the drape of contemporary global menswear.
- **Finishing:** genuine luxury execution — embroidery, puff printing, DTF, appliqué, hand-finishing, weighted trims, considered interiors, premium hardware.
- **Restraint:** luxury whispers. We use heritage as depth, not as loudness. One perfect story per garment beats ten motifs fighting.

The test: it must feel expensive *before* you know the story, and priceless *after*.

### III. Storytelling Fashion

We sell meaning. Cloth is how we deliver it.

- Every collection is a **chapter of India**, not a "drop."
- Every garment carries a **narrative** — the art form, the region, the myth, the maker.
- The story travels with the product: on the website, in the packaging, on the woven label, in the hang-tag that reads like a page from a book.

A NAVARN customer can never answer "nice shirt, what is it?" with just a brand name. He answers with a story. That is the entire point.

# 4. VISUAL IDENTITY SYSTEM

The visual system exists to signal two things at once, in every frame: **India** and **luxury**. If a viewer sees only one, the frame has failed.

### Color Palette

Heritage tones, never neon. The world is drawn from Indian pigment, metal, earth and ink.

**Core**
- `Ink Black` #0E0E0C — the void, the gallery wall, authority.
- `Heritage Ivory` #F4EDE1 — aged paper, cotton, the canvas of the artisan.
- `Antique Gold` #B08D4C — the metal of the crown; used with extreme restraint, never plated-shiny.

**Heritage Accents** (one leads per collection — this is how collections become chromatically distinct)
- `Indigo` #2A3A5E — Warli / Kalamkari night, dye vats.
- `Terracotta` #A8452E — earth, the Warli wall, Gond soil.
- `Madder Madhubani Red` #7E2B22 — myth, festival, life.
- `Deep Kalamkari Teal` #17453F — vegetal dye, botanical depth.
- `Sindoor / Warrior Crimson` #8C1C1C — fire, valour, blood memory.
- `Bone / Undyed Cotton` #E7DBC6 — craft, honesty, the raw ground.

**Rule:** ~80% neutral (ink / ivory / bone), ~15% single collection accent, ~5% gold. Gold is seasoning, never the dish. Streetwear screams in full color; NAVARN speaks in ink and lets one heritage tone glow.

### Typography

A dialogue between the ancient and the now — exactly the brand's tension made legible.

- **Display / Headlines — a high-contrast refined serif** (Didone or transitional lineage; e.g. a *Canela*, *Ogg*, *GT Sectra*, or a bespoke wordmark serif). This is the *authority and heritage* voice. Used large, with generous space, for chapter titles and the NAVARN name.
- **Body / Interface — a clean, humanist or grotesque sans** (e.g. *Neue Haas / Suisse / Söhne / Aeonik*). This is the *modernity* voice. Quiet, precise, invisible in the best way.
- **Accent / Editorial — an italic serif** for pull-quotes and story fragments, to make the storytelling feel *written*, literary, curated.

**Typographic laws:** enormous whitespace; small, confident type over huge margins; wide letter-spacing on labels (a museum-caption feel); left-aligned editorial ragged text for the story chapters. Never crowd. Luxury is the space around the word as much as the word.

### Logo Direction

- **Wordmark-led.** NAVARN set in the display serif — refined, high-contrast, timeless. This is the primary asset and should feel like it could be embossed on a book of history or a museum plaque.
- **A monogram / seal** — an "N" or emblem that behaves like a *maker's seal / royal insignia* — for the woven label, the button, the hang-tag wax-seal, the favicon, the embroidery. The seal is the "keeper's mark."
- **Optional motif system:** a fine-line iconographic language derived from the collections (a Warli sun, a Gond dot-field, a horse) — used sparingly as a supergraphic, never as clutter.
- **Feel:** if Cartier and a national museum co-signed a document, the logo is the letterhead. Engraved, not printed. Confident, not loud.

### Photography Style

The photograph must feel like a **portrait in a gallery**, not a lookbook and never a catalog.

- **Editorial, cinematic, sculptural.** Single strong subject. Rembrandt / chiaroscuro lighting — one directional source, deep controlled shadow, the garment and the man emerging from darkness.
- **Muted, filmic grade** — desaturated toward ink and ivory, one heritage tone allowed to warm the frame. Grain, not gloss.
- **The man is rooted, still, sovereign** — never mid-jump, never "hype." Stillness reads as luxury and as pride.
- **Context that honors origin:** raw plaster walls, temple stone, aged paper, dye vats, the artisan's hand and the modern city — juxtaposed, so heritage and now share the frame.
- **Macro craft shots are non-negotiable** — the rise of puff, the density of thread, the tooth of the fabric. We prove the luxury; we don't assert it.

### Cinematic Style

Film is where NAVARN becomes a house, not a label. The cinematic language is **heritage documentary meets luxury campaign meets myth**.

- **Grade:** filmic, warm-shadowed, low-key. Think *anamorphic, 35mm, tungsten-and-shadow*. Dust in light. Ink black backgrounds.
- **Pace:** slow. Long holds. The camera reveres. Slow push-ins on a hand painting, a needle, an eye, a fabric moving in wind.
- **Structure:** always intercut two worlds — the *ancient* (artisan, wall, pigment, ritual) and the *modern* (the man, the city, the garment) — so the transformation is the story.
- **Sound:** a low sustained drone; a single classical Indian instrument (sarangi, bansuri, been) treated cinematically; the diegetic sound of craft — brush, thread, fire — foregrounded. Silence used as luxury.
- **No hype edits. No fast cuts. No trend audio.** If it could be a sneaker ad, kill it.

### Motion Language

How NAVARN moves — on screen, on the site, in animation.

- **Weighted, slow, deliberate.** Everything eases in and settles like heavy silk, never bounces. Custom easing (long, luxurious cubic-beziers), nothing springy or playful.
- **Reveal, don't perform.** Type and image *emerge* — fades, slow rises, gentle mask-wipes like ink spreading or a curtain lifting. Heritage is uncovered, not thrown at you.
- **Cinematic scroll.** The screen behaves like film — one idea at a time, held, then it gives way. Parallax used with restraint to add depth, never dizziness.
- **Ink & pigment as transition metaphor** — sections dissolve like dye in water, wipe like a brushstroke, fill like gold leaf.
- **Rule of stillness:** motion earns its place. When in doubt, hold. Luxury is a house confident enough to be still.

# 5. DESIGN LANGUAGE

### How NAVARN Should Feel

Like walking into a dimly lit private gallery where every object is lit like a relic, a low classical drone hums, and a curator who happens to dress like a king turns to you and begins, quietly: *"Let me show you something your grandfather's grandfather knew."*

- **Reverent** — the hush of a museum, the weight of a temple.
- **Sovereign** — the calm authority of old power; nothing has to shout.
- **Warm and human** — the hand, the thread, the maker; never cold minimalism for its own sake.
- **Cinematic** — you are inside a film about your own heritage.
- **Rare** — spacious, unhurried, withholding — the opposite of a crowded marketplace.

The governing tension in *everything*: **ancient ↔ modern**, **ink ↔ gold**, **stillness ↔ life**, **reverence ↔ swagger**.

### What Emotions the Customer Should Experience

In sequence, as intended:

1. **Awe** — "I did not know this was ours."
2. **Pride** — "This is mine. This is *us*." (the core emotion; everything serves it)
3. **Belonging** — "I am part of a lineage." (the antidote to a foreign logo)
4. **Reverence** — "This deserves to be preserved."
5. **Desire** — "I want to wear this. I want to carry this story."
6. **Distinction** — "Wearing this makes me rare, rooted, and known."

The final feeling at checkout is not "I bought a shirt." It is **"I became a keeper."**

# 6. COLLECTION ARCHITECTURE

Each collection is a **chapter of India**. All collections inherit one narrative template:
**Origin (state / art form) → The Story → The Emotion → Visual Language → Materials → Technique → Packaging.**

Grouped in three houses:
- **The Folk-Art Houses** (living tribal & regional art forms): Warli, Madhubani, Kalamkari, Gond, Pattachitra
- **The Legacy Houses** (royalty, valour, spirit, myth): Maharaja, Warrior, Untamed Horse, Fire, Mythology
- **The Keystone**: 28 States Heritage Collection

---

### 6.1 WARLI — *The First Language*

- **Story:** From the walls of Maharashtra's Warli tribe come the oldest visual grammar of India — humans, harvest, dance and the sacred, drawn in white rice-paste circles and triangles on earth-red walls. Before writing, we drew. NAVARN's founding chapter returns to that first language: the tarpa dance, the wedding, the tree of life.
- **Emotional positioning:** Origin. Innocence. The beginning of everything. *"This is where the story of India begins."*
- **Visual language:** Terracotta ground, rice-white line. Minimal, geometric, meditative. Vast negative space around small dense figure-clusters.
- **Materials:** Heavyweight earth-red and undyed raw cotton; matte, textured hand-feel.
- **Technique:** Fine white line-work in high-density DTF for crisp geometry; key motifs raised in tonal white puff for tactility; a hero figure in white thread embroidery.
- **Packaging:** Recycled earth-toned board box, letterpressed white Warli line, wrapped in unbleached cotton and tied with jute; a folded story-card printed like a wall fragment.

---

### 6.2 MADHUBANI — *The Painted Prayer*

- **Story:** From Mithila, Bihar — painted for centuries by women on the walls and floors of their homes for weddings and gods. Fish for fertility, peacocks for love, the sun and lotus for the divine; every inch filled, because a prayer leaves no space empty. NAVARN carries the household devotion of Mithila onto the shoulders of the modern man.
- **Emotional positioning:** Devotion, celebration, feminine sacred energy, abundance. *"A prayer you can wear."*
- **Visual language:** Dense double-line figures, riotous-but-controlled madder red, ochre, indigo, black. Maximal, festive, symmetrical.
- **Materials:** Smooth mid-weight cotton to hold fine detail; ivory and madder-red grounds.
- **Technique:** High-detail DTF for line density; selective embroidery on eyes, fish and borders; metallic gold thread accents on the divine motifs.
- **Packaging:** Deep madder-red box, gold-foil fish seal, lined with block-print tissue; incense-note story card; a small painted detail reproduced as a collectible art insert.

---

### 6.3 KALAMKARI — *The Pen of the Storyteller*

- **Story:** *Kalam* (pen) + *kari* (craft) — the Andhra/Telangana art of drawing epics with a bamboo pen and natural dyes, for temple scrolls that narrated the Ramayana and Mahabharata to the unlettered. It is literally *storytelling-as-textile* — NAVARN's philosophy made ancient. Botanical scrolls, deities, the tree of life in vegetal indigo and madder.
- **Emotional positioning:** Narrative, wisdom, the epic, the sacred botanical. *"Every line is a verse."*
- **Visual language:** Fine flowing hand-drawn line, indigo and deep teal on cream, intricate borders, mythic scenes.
- **Materials:** Fine cotton and cotton-silk with a natural-dye matte hand.
- **Technique:** Ultra-fine DTF line reproduction; tonal embroidery on borders; hand-finished natural-dye-effect grounds.
- **Packaging:** Scroll-inspired packaging — the garment rolled, not folded, wrapped in a printed Kalamkari-border cloth, tied with cord and a wax seal; story-card as a mini temple scroll.

---

### 6.4 GOND — *The Song of the Forest*

- **Story:** From the Gond people of central India — the forest sung into being. Trees, deer, birds and spirits built from thousands of fine dots and dashes, each pattern a signature of rhythm. Their belief: seeing a good image brings good luck. NAVARN wears the forest and its luck.
- **Emotional positioning:** Life, rhythm, nature, animist spirit, vitality. *"The forest, alive on your back."*
- **Visual language:** Bold silhouetted animals and trees filled with intricate dot-and-line pattern; vivid earth-and-jewel tones on dark ground.
- **Materials:** Deep-dyed heavyweight cotton (forest green, ink, ochre); substantial, structured.
- **Technique:** Silhouette in flat DTF, internal dot-fields in fine print, key creature raised in puff and outlined in embroidery for dimensional life.
- **Packaging:** Forest-dark box, embossed dot-pattern texture, seeded/plantable story card (the forest returns to the earth), pressed-leaf detail.

---

### 6.5 PATTACHITRA — *The Cloth of the Gods*

- **Story:** From Odisha — *patta* (cloth) + *chitra* (picture) — sacred scroll paintings of Jagannath and the epics, made on treated cloth with natural pigment and legendary decorative borders. A temple art of astonishing discipline. NAVARN inherits its divine ornamentation and iconic figure-work.
- **Emotional positioning:** The sacred, the ornamental, divine order, discipline. *"Cloth once reserved for the gods."*
- **Visual language:** Bold figures, expressive eyes, ornate floral borders, warm natural palette — red, yellow, black, white — flat and iconic.
- **Materials:** Structured cotton-canvas with a treated, slightly stiff scroll-like hand; ivory and ochre grounds.
- **Technique:** DTF for iconography and border precision; embroidery and gold-thread on borders and jewelry of figures; puff on decorative frames.
- **Packaging:** Cloth-wrapped like a consecrated scroll, tied and sealed; a printed border frames the story-card like a devotional panel; a small brass-tone seal.

---

### 6.6 MAHARAJA — *The Weight of a Crown*

- **Story:** The courts of India's kings — Rajput, Mughal-influenced, princely — where power dressed in brocade, miniature portraiture, jewels and durbar splendor. NAVARN distills the *bearing* of the Maharaja: not costume, but the calm authority of one born to rule, translated into modern sovereign menswear.
- **Emotional positioning:** Power, legacy, sovereignty, inherited command. *"Dress like the throne remembers you."* (the brand's Ruler archetype in its purest form)
- **Visual language:** Miniature-portrait motifs, royal crest, elephant and durbar iconography, deep jewel tones with gold; ornate but disciplined.
- **Materials:** The most luxurious of the line — heavyweight cotton, cotton-silk, considered interiors, premium hardware; deep maroon, midnight, ink with gold.
- **Technique:** The full craft arsenal — dense gold-thread embroidery, appliqué, puff crest-work, DTF miniature detail, metallic finishing.
- **Packaging:** The flagship box — rigid, cloth-bound, magnetic, gold-foil crest, silk-feel lining, wax-sealed certificate of the story, a woven royal label; presented like a gift from a court.

---

### 6.7 WARRIOR — *The Vow of Valour*

- **Story:** The warriors of India — Rajput, Maratha, Sikh, Ahom and the countless unnamed — bound by a vow: honour over life. Shields, blades, banners and the disciplined ferocity of men who guarded a civilization. NAVARN honours the *code*, not the violence — courage, sacrifice, standing your ground.
- **Emotional positioning:** Valour, discipline, honour, strength, resolve. *"Some things are worth standing for."*
- **Visual language:** Armour and weapon motifs, battle standards, bold heraldic composition; crimson, steel, ink and iron.
- **Materials:** Rugged heavyweight cotton with a battle-worn, structured hand; darker, harder palette.
- **Technique:** Puff for armour and relief; metallic and steel-tone thread embroidery; distressed DTF for a weathered, forged texture.
- **Packaging:** Iron-grey armour-textured box, embossed crest, a "vow card" printed like an oath, wrapped in heavy raw cloth, a metal-tone seal.

---

### 6.8 UNTAMED HORSE — *The Spirit That Cannot Be Held*

- **Story:** The horse across Indian memory — the Marwari's inward-curved ears, the warrior's mount, the terracotta votive horses of village shrines, the steed of gods. It is the eternal Indian symbol of freedom, nobility and untamed spirit. NAVARN's most emotive, energetic chapter: raw movement, mane and dust.
- **Emotional positioning:** Freedom, wild nobility, unbroken spirit, motion. *"Freedom was always the point."*
- **Visual language:** Dynamic horse in motion — mane, dust, muscle — expressive and kinetic against still ground; ink, bone, terracotta.
- **Materials:** Fluid, movement-friendly heavyweight cotton with drape; monochrome-leaning grounds so the horse commands.
- **Technique:** Expressive DTF for movement and texture, mane and key musculature raised in puff, thread embroidery for the flying mane — dimension that reads as motion.
- **Packaging:** Bone-toned box, a single galloping horse embossed edge-to-edge, raw twine, dust-paper wrap; story-card printed like a field sketch.

---

### 6.9 FIRE — *The First God*

- **Story:** *Agni* — fire, India's oldest deity, the witness of every vow, the carrier of every offering, the flame of the sacred lamp and the funeral pyre; transformation itself. NAVARN's most abstract, elemental, cinematic chapter — the force that turns one thing into another. (It is also our metaphor: the fire that transforms heritage into fashion.)
- **Emotional positioning:** Transformation, passion, the sacred and dangerous, energy. *"Everything worth having passes through fire."*
- **Visual language:** Flame forms, sacred lamp, ember gradients, Agni iconography; deep ink grounds with crimson, ember-orange and gold rising from the dark.
- **Materials:** Ink-black and charcoal heavyweight cotton, deep and matte, so the fire glows.
- **Technique:** Gradient DTF for ember glow, flame edges raised in puff, gold-thread embroidery for the sacred flame — the fire literally rises off the surface.
- **Packaging:** Charred-black box with an ember-gold inner reveal, a matchbox-inspired sleeve or lamp motif, a story-card that reads like a hymn to Agni, a gold wax seal.

---

### 6.10 MYTHOLOGY — *The Endless Epic*

- **Story:** The gods and epics — the Ramayana, the Mahabharata, the Puranas — the deepest well of story any civilization possesses. Divine forms, cosmic events, symbols that have guided a billion lives. NAVARN's grandest, most reverent chapter: myth rendered with the gravity it deserves, never trivialized.
- **Emotional positioning:** The eternal, the cosmic, the profound, awe. *"The stories that made us."*
- **Visual language:** Divine iconography rendered with restraint and reverence — silhouette, symbol and detail over literal portraiture; deep cosmic palette, indigo-black, gold, sacred red.
- **Materials:** Premium heavyweight cotton and cotton-silk; deep, serious grounds with luxury finishing.
- **Technique:** The full arsenal used with restraint — fine DTF detail, gold-thread divine embroidery, subtle puff halos, metallic finishing; reverence over spectacle.
- **Packaging:** Deep-indigo cloth-bound box, gold cosmic seal, a story-card written like a verse of scripture, tissue printed with a subtle cosmic map; treated as a sacred object.

---

### 6.11 28 STATES HERITAGE COLLECTION — *One Nation, Woven*

- **Story:** The keystone and the mission made visible. A living, expanding canon — one signature garment for the heritage of every Indian state and union territory, each a distilled portrait of its land, art, symbol and soul. Not a season but a *national project* NAVARN commits to completing. To collect across it is to wear the map of a civilization.
- **Emotional positioning:** Unity, ambition, national pride, belonging, completeness. *"Wear the map of a civilization."* — the collection that proves the mission is real.
- **Visual language:** A consistent editorial system — each state as a numbered, curated plate (state emblem, one defining art/symbol, a signature heritage tone) so the set reads as one canon while each garment stays distinct. Museum-catalog discipline.
- **Materials:** Signature NAVARN heavyweight cotton across the set for cohesion, with each state's accent tone and finishing detail.
- **Technique:** A shared craft language (DTF + puff + embroidery) applied per state, with a consistent "state seal" embroidered mark that unifies the collection.
- **Packaging:** A unified collector system — each state boxed identically save for its number, emblem and tone, designed to sit together on a shelf as a growing library; an included "map card" tracks which states the owner has collected. Built for the completist and the patriot.

# 7. WEBSITE STORYTELLING FRAMEWORK

*(Framework and art direction only — no code, no build. This is the cinematic screenplay the site will one day be built from.)*

The website is not a store. It is a **film you scroll through** — a cinematic descent from India's forgotten stories to the garment on the man's back, ending at the threshold of ownership. It opens in ink-black silence and slowly fills with heritage, warmth and gold. Commerce appears only after the customer has been *moved* — never before.

**Global art direction:** ink-black canvas, editorial serif titles emerging from darkness, weighted slow motion, dye/ink/gold-leaf transitions, low classical drone, one idea per screen, held. The scroll *reveres*. The store is the last room, not the front door.

---

### Chapter 1 — The Lost Stories of India

- **Purpose / emotion:** Awe, then a pang of loss. *"I did not know this was ours — and I almost lost it."*
- **Experience:** Open in near-black. Fragments of ancient art — a Warli line, a Gond dot-field, a Kalamkari scroll — drift up out of the dark and fade like fading memory. Sparse, reverent serif lines appear one at a time: *"Three thousand years of stories. Nearly forgotten in three generations."* No product. No menu clutter. A held silence.
- **Craft cue:** ink-in-water reveals; dust in a single beam of light; the drone begins.

### Chapter 2 — The Artisans

- **Purpose / emotion:** Reverence and human warmth. Establishes authenticity and the preservation contract. *"Real hands kept this alive."*
- **Experience:** The dark warms. Slow cinematic macro of hands — the bamboo pen, the needle, the rice-paste brush, the dye vat. Portraits of makers, named, lit like relics. Quiet text on the communities and the vanishing knowledge. This is where trust is earned and appropriation is answered — with faces and credit.
- **Craft cue:** long holds, diegetic craft sound foregrounded, warm low-key light.

### Chapter 3 — The Transformation

- **Purpose / emotion:** The reveal, the "aha," desire igniting. *"This ancient thing just became mine to wear."* The Fire chapter's metaphor, literalized.
- **Experience:** The pivot of the film. On a split or morph, the ancient wall / scroll *becomes* the modern garment — the Warli line resolves into embroidery on a shoulder, the dye becomes fabric, the pigment becomes puff. Macro craft shots prove the luxury: the rise of the puff, the density of thread. Ancient and modern share one frame at last.
- **Craft cue:** the signature dissolve — pigment-into-textile morph; gold leaf fills the NAVARN seal.

### Chapter 4 — The Heritage Collections

- **Purpose / emotion:** Immersion and desire, structured as a gallery. *"Which story is mine?"*
- **Experience:** The house opens its wings. Each collection is a **cinematic room** the user enters — full-bleed hero, the collection's single accent color washing the screen, its one-line myth, its craft. Warli's terracotta, Fire's ember-black, Maharaja's gold. Navigable like a museum floor, not a product grid. The 28 States collection presented as the grand hall — the growing national canon. Product exists here, but framed as *artifacts*, each with its story.
- **Craft cue:** each room transitions via its collection's material metaphor (scroll unrolls, dye spreads, fire rises); price and cart are present but quiet, subordinate to story.

### Chapter 5 — The Modern Indian Man

- **Purpose / emotion:** Identification and belonging. *"This is who I am now."* The customer sees himself.
- **Experience:** The film turns from heritage to the man who carries it. Editorial portraits — the rooted, sovereign modern Indian man in the city, still and commanding, wearing the stories. Ancient context and modern life share the frame. Voice/text: heritage is not the past you visit, it is the identity you wear. This is where pride converts to self-image.
- **Craft cue:** chiaroscuro portraiture, city-meets-heritage juxtaposition, stillness as power.

### Chapter 6 — Wear Your Heritage

- **Purpose / emotion:** Resolve, invitation, ownership — the threshold. *"I want to become a keeper."*
- **Experience:** The film lands. The NAVARN seal in gold on ink. The mission, stated plainly: *"To preserve India's stories through premium fashion."* The single call to action — *Wear Your Heritage* — opens into the full collections / store. Only now does commerce fully arrive, and the customer arrives at it already *moved*, already belonging. Checkout should feel like being handed a relic and a title, not completing a transaction.
- **Craft cue:** everything settles to stillness; gold seal; the drone resolves; the last screen holds.

## APPENDIX — LAWS OF THE HOUSE (quick reference)

1. **Story-first, always.** No garment ships without a reason to exist.
2. **India *and* luxury in every frame** — never only one.
3. **Craft is proven, not claimed** — show the puff, the thread, the weight.
4. **Reverence over appropriation** — source, honor, credit the origin.
5. **Ink and space over color and clutter** — 80% neutral, one accent, gold as seasoning.
