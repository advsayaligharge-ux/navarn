# NAVARN — VISUAL IDENTITY BIBLE

### The Complete Visual System of India's Luxury Heritage Menswear House

> Built on the official NAVARN mark. This document governs every color, letterform, frame, box and pixel. Where this bible refines the earlier `BRAND_DNA.md` color notes, **this document is authoritative** — the official logo now anchors the palette.

**The visual law of the house:** every surface must say two things at once — **India** and **luxury**. Emerald and gold is not decoration here; it is the palette of the Mughal and Rajput jeweled courts. We are dressing the modern man in the color of kings.

## 1. LOGO ANALYSIS

### 1.1 The Mark

The NAVARN identity is a **monogram-and-wordmark lockup**:

- **The Emblem** — a stylized **"N"** built from two vertical pillar-forms bridged by a bold diagonal, rendered as a **champagne-to-antique-gold outline** enclosing a **deep emerald-green field**, with fine gold rules breaking the interior into horizontal bands. The silhouette rounds at the top-right and bottom-left into a soft **shield / seal** form.
- **The Wordmark** — `NAVARN` in a clean, wide-tracked geometric sans, set in **midnight navy**, all caps, engineered and confident.

### 1.2 Symbolism — What the Mark Says

This is not an abstract "N." It is a compressed statement of the entire brand. Read it in four layers:

1. **The Pillars — Heritage & Permanence.** The two upright forms read as **temple columns / architectural pillars**: stability, lineage, stories that *stand*. NAVARN as a house built to last, not a trend that passes.
2. **The Horizontal Bands — The Loom.** The interior gold rules read as **woven threads on a loom** — the literal act of a textile house. Stories being *woven into cloth*. For a fashion brand whose mission is preservation, the fabric is written into the logo itself.
3. **The Diagonal — Transformation.** The bold stroke cutting across the classical pillars is **the modern gesture** — *nav*, the new. The ancient (pillars) and the modern (the slash) meet inside one letter. The brand's central tension — **ancient made new** — is encoded in the geometry.
4. **The Shield / Seal — The Keeper's Mark.** The rounded, tablet-like silhouette behaves like a **crest, a royal insignia, a maker's seal** — the Sage/Ruler archetype made into a stamp. This is what gets embossed, embroidered and wax-sealed.

**Color symbolism:**
- **Emerald green** — the royal Indian gemstone (the emeralds of Mughal and Rajput treasuries), prosperity, the land, living heritage. Rare, deep, sovereign.
- **Champagne & antique gold** — royalty, the metal of the crown, aged luxury, the leaf on a manuscript. Warm, not brash.
- **Midnight navy** — authority, timelessness, the night sky of myth. Grounds the gold and green in seriousness.

The mark is **regal, architectural and textile-literate** all at once — an exceptionally strong foundation.

### 1.3 Recommended Refinements

The mark is strong. These are *productionizing* refinements, not redesigns — to make it survive at every scale and on every material a luxury house touches.

1. **Build a version ladder (mandatory).** The gradient-gold-on-emerald hero mark is beautiful on screen but will muddy in foil, embroidery and small sizes. Commission these locked variants:
   - **Primary** — gradient gold + emerald (digital hero, large format).
   - **Flat two-color** — solid antique gold + emerald (print, packaging).
   - **Single-color gold** — for foil-stamping and embossing.
   - **Single-color emerald / navy / charcoal** — for mono contexts.
   - **Reversed / ivory** — knockout on dark and on emerald.
   - **Embroidery mark** — simplified, thicker minimum stroke; the fine interior rules merged or dropped so thread can hold it on a woven label.
2. **Lock one emerald.** Fix a single reproducible `Emerald` value (below) so the green never drifts between suppliers.
3. **Emblem-only seal.** Formalize the emblem *without* the wordmark as the **"Keeper's Seal"** — the icon for buttons, hang-tag wax seals, embroidered garment marks, favicon and social avatar.
4. **Wordmark alternates.** Provide the wordmark in **navy, ink-charcoal, gold, and ivory** so the lockup adapts to any ground.
5. **Optional monochrome elegance.** For the most editorial luxury contexts (campaign end-frames, embossed stationery), an **all-gold** or **tone-on-tone** lockup often out-classes the full-color version. Keep it in the kit.

### 1.4 Logo Usage Rules

**Lockups**
- **Vertical (primary):** emblem centered above wordmark — the hero lockup.
- **Emblem-only (seal):** for small, tactile, or repeated applications.
- **Horizontal:** emblem left of wordmark — for narrow headers and labels only.

**Clear space**
- Minimum clear space on all sides = **the width of one pillar** of the emblem. Nothing — type, edge, image, another logo — intrudes.

**Minimum sizes**
- Emblem: **24 px** digital / **10 mm** print (use the simplified embroidery version below this).
- Full vertical lockup: **48 px** digital / **20 mm** print.

**Approved grounds**
- Ivory / Royal Cream (primary), Deep Charcoal, Emerald, Midnight Navy, and clean neutral photography with adequate contrast.

**Do NOT**
- ❌ Recolor the emblem outside the approved variant set.
- ❌ Place the gradient gold over busy or low-contrast imagery — use the reversed/flat version.
- ❌ Stretch, skew, rotate, add drop shadows, glows or bevels beyond the built-in gold gradient.
- ❌ Re-tint the emerald toward lime, teal or olive.
- ❌ Crowd the clear space or shrink below minimums.
- ❌ Set the wordmark in any other typeface, or re-track it.
- ❌ Use the mark on cheap, glossy, or neon grounds — anything that reads streetwear.

**Foil & embroidery**
- On packaging: gold **foil-stamp or emboss** the emblem; do not print flat gold ink (it looks cheap).
- On garments/labels: emblem in **gold or tonal thread**; use the thickened embroidery variant.

## 3. TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM

A dialogue between **the manuscript and the machine** — heritage authority meets modern precision. Four voices, each with one job.

### 3.1 Display Font — *The Voice of Heritage*
- **Character:** high-contrast refined serif (Didone / transitional lineage) — the letterform of a museum plaque and a book of history.
- **Recommended:** **Canela**, **Ogg**, or **GT Sectra** (alt: **Freight Display**, **Noe Display**).
- **Use:** chapter titles, hero statements, the largest editorial moments. Set **large, with vast space and tight leading**. This is where NAVARN sounds *ancient and sovereign*.

### 3.2 Editorial Font — *The Voice of the Storyteller*
- **Character:** an **italic / roman serif** with literary warmth — makes the storytelling feel *written*, curated, hand-set.
- **Recommended:** **Canela Text** or **Lyon Text** (italics for pull-quotes and story fragments).
- **Use:** collection myths, pull-quotes, story-card prose, "curator's note" passages. The reverent, literary register.

### 3.3 Body Font — *The Voice of the Modern House*
- **Character:** clean, quiet, neutral grotesque/humanist sans — invisible in the best way, carrying the wordmark's engineered feel.
- **Recommended:** **Söhne**, **Suisse Int'l**, or **Neue Haas Grotesk** (alt: **Aeonik**).
- **Use:** body copy, navigation, product detail, UI, captions. **Wide tracking on labels** for the museum-caption feel. This is the *modernity* half of the brand.

### 3.4 Luxury Numerals — *The Voice of Value*
- **Character:** **oldstyle (text-figure) numerals** from the serif family for editorial contexts (dates, collection numbers, "State No. 07"), and **tabular lining figures** from the sans for prices and specs.
- **Rule:** prices are set **quiet and small** in the sans — never enlarged, never bold, never shouted. In a luxury house the number is stated, not sold. Currency mark small, generous space.
- **Signature detail:** collection and state numbers set as **Roman numerals** in the display serif for the most ceremonial contexts (e.g. *Chapter III*, *State No. XII*).

### 3.5 Typographic Laws
- Enormous whitespace; small confident type over huge margins.
- Display serif for emotion, sans for information — **never** mix their jobs.
- Wide letter-spacing (tracking) on all-caps labels; the wordmark's DNA extended to the system.
- Left-aligned, ragged editorial text for story chapters; centered only for ceremonial title moments.
- Two type sizes per view where possible — restraint reads as luxury.

## 4. MOTION LANGUAGE

Motion in NAVARN is **weighted, reverent and cinematic** — heavy silk, not rubber. Nothing bounces; everything settles.

### 4.1 Animation Style
- **Weighted & slow.** Long, luxurious easing (custom cubic-bezier, deep ease-out; ~600–1200 ms for meaningful reveals). No springs, no bounces, nothing playful.
- **Reveal, don't perform.** Type and image **emerge** — fades, slow upward rises, gentle mask-wipes. Heritage is *uncovered*, never thrown at the viewer.
- **Gold as light.** Interactive accents (links, the seal, hover states) glow with a slow champagne-gold shift, like light moving across metal — never a hard color flip.

### 4.2 Camera Movement
- **Slow push-ins and drifts.** The camera reveres its subject — a long, patient dolly toward a hand, a thread, an eye, the fabric moving in wind.
- **Locked, composed frames.** Stillness dominates; movement is deliberate and rare. No handheld shake, no whip-pans, no hype energy.
- **Parallax with restraint** — subtle depth between foreground craft and background ground; never dizzying.

### 4.3 Transition Language
- **The signature: pigment & gold.** Sections dissolve like **ink/dye spreading in water**; the mark and titles **fill like gold leaf**; collection rooms transition via their own material metaphor (a scroll unrolls, dye blooms, fire rises, dust settles).
- **Masked reveals** — a brushstroke or curtain wipe uncovers the next frame.
- **Cross-dissolves over cuts** — the film breathes between ideas; hard cuts are reserved for rare, intentional impact.

### 4.4 Scroll Behavior
- **Cinematic, one idea per screen.** The page behaves like film: a moment arrives, is **held**, then gives way. Generous scroll distance per beat.
- **Scroll-linked reveals** — copy and imagery animate to scroll position with heavy easing; pinned "chapters" hold while their story completes.
- **The rule of stillness** — motion must earn its place. When in doubt, hold. A house confident enough to be still *is* the luxury signal.

## 5. PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION

Every image is a **portrait in a gallery** — never a catalog, never a lookbook, never "hype."

### 5.1 Lighting
- **Chiaroscuro / Rembrandt.** One directional source, deep controlled shadow; the subject and garment **emerge from darkness**. Emerald and charcoal grounds swallow the shadow; a single warm key models the form.
- **Low-key and filmic** — rich blacks, no flat fill, no beauty-dish gloss. Light like a Mughal miniature lit by a single lamp.
- **Warm shadow, cool control** — shadows carry a faint warmth (candle, gold); highlights stay disciplined.

### 5.2 Composition
- **One strong subject, centered or classically placed** (rule-of-thirds, generous negative space).
- **Stillness = sovereignty.** The man is rooted, upright, composed — never mid-motion. Pride reads as *calm*.
- **Macro craft frames are mandatory** — the rise of the puff, the density of thread, the tooth of the fabric, the gold on a button. We *prove* luxury.
- **Two-world framing** — ancient context (plaster wall, temple stone, dye vat, aged paper) shares the frame with the modern man and garment.

### 5.3 Editorial Style
- **Muted, filmic grade** — desaturated toward cream, charcoal and emerald; one collection accent allowed to warm the frame. **Grain, not gloss.** 35mm / medium-format feel.
- **Museum-caption treatment** — images paired with small, wide-tracked captions like gallery labels (art form, region, craft).
- **The maker present** — the artisan's hands and face appear as reverently as the model's; authenticity is shown, not claimed.

### 5.4 Fashion Campaign Style
- **Cinematic, sculptural, sovereign.** Think a fine-art fashion film crossed with a heritage documentary — the man as a modern king in a still, dark, gold-touched world.
- **Anamorphic, tungsten-and-shadow** stills that could hang framed. Emerald and gold recur as the campaign's chromatic signature.
- **Never:** mid-jump energy, neon, flat e-commerce white-box as *campaign* imagery (clean packshots exist only in the utility/PDP layer, kept visually separate from the editorial world).

## 6. PACKAGING SYSTEM

Packaging is the **first physical proof** of the house. Unboxing must feel like being handed a relic and a title — a ceremony, not a delivery. Materials: rigid board, cloth binding, uncoated heritage stock, gold foil, wax, jute and cotton.

### 6.1 The Luxury Box
- **Rigid, cloth-bound magnetic box** in Deep Charcoal or Emerald, with the **Keeper's Seal gold-foil-stamped / embossed** on the lid (foil or emboss only — never printed gold).
- **Interior** lined in Royal Cream or the collection accent, with a soft matte finish; garment wrapped in printed heritage tissue and sealed with a **gold or emerald wax seal**.
- **Ribbon pull** in tonal cotton; the box opens like a book of history.
- **Tiered:** flagship collections (Maharaja, Mythology, 28 States) receive the full cloth-bound magnetic box; core collections receive a refined rigid box with foil seal — always premium, never plastic, never glossy.

### 6.2 Tags (Hang-Tags)
- **Thick uncoated parchment stock**, letterpressed or foil-blocked with the emblem.
- **Two-part:** a front **crest side** (emblem + collection name + Roman-numeral collection number) and a reverse **micro-story** (one or two lines from the collection myth).
- Attached with **jute cord and a small brass-tone seal or bead**, not plastic loops.

### 6.3 Story Cards
- **A folded card, printed like a page from a book of heritage**, one per garment. Contents: the art form, its region and community, the myth, and the craft used. Editorial serif prose on parchment stock, accent-color detail per collection.
- Doubles as a **collectible art insert** — a reproduced detail of the source art on the reverse, suitable to keep or frame.

### 6.4 Certificates
- **A Certificate of Heritage** — a single ceremonial sheet on heavy parchment, foil emblem, wide-tracked serif, giving the garment's collection, its story, the artisan community credited, and a **numbered edition / seal**.
- Signed/sealed with the wax **Keeper's Seal**. For 28 States pieces, it records the **State number and name** and functions as the collector's proof.
- This is the physical embodiment of *"you became a keeper."*

### 6.5 Garment Bags
- **Reusable cotton / raw-canvas dust bag** in Bone or Emerald, screen-printed or embroidered with the tonal emblem — protective, sustainable, and a lasting brand object in the wardrobe.
- Drawstring in tonal cotton cord with a brass tip; interior stamp with the mission line: *"To preserve India's stories through premium fashion."*

*Packaging law: nothing plastic, nothing glossy, nothing loud. Every component is uncoated, tactile, foil-or-embossed, and reusable. The waste stream should feel like heritage too.*

## 7. WEBSITE VISUAL DIRECTION

*(Art direction and mood only — no code, no pages, per instruction. This is the visual brief the future build must honor.)*

### 7.1 Cinematic Mood Boards (the six visual worlds)

Aligned to the six-chapter storytelling framework, each with its own visual key:

1. **The Lost Stories** — near-black charcoal void; fragments of ancient art (Warli line, Gond dots, Kalamkari scroll) drifting up out of darkness and fading; single beam of dusty light; the emblem barely glinting in gold. *Mood: awe and loss.*
2. **The Artisans** — warm low-key macro of hands, pen, needle, dye vat; named portraits lit like relics; parchment and bone tones. *Mood: reverence, humanity.*
3. **The Transformation** — the pivot: ancient wall/scroll morphing into embroidered garment; emerald and gold igniting; macro of puff and thread; gold-leaf fill on the seal. *Mood: the reveal, desire.*
4. **The Collections** — a series of cinematic "rooms," each washed in its collection accent over the constant emerald/gold/ivory architecture; artifacts, not products. *Mood: immersion, curation.*
5. **The Modern Indian Man** — chiaroscuro editorial portraits; city-meets-heritage; the sovereign, still figure wearing the stories; emerald, gold, charcoal. *Mood: identification, belonging.*
6. **Wear Your Heritage** — resolve to stillness; the gold Keeper's Seal on charcoal; the mission line; the single invitation into the collections. *Mood: ownership, the threshold.*

### 7.2 Art Direction
- **Ground:** Deep Charcoal and Emerald dominate the cinematic chapters; Royal Cream / Ivory for the editorial and shopping layers — a deliberate move from *dark reverence* to *warm daylight* as the customer descends from myth toward garment.
- **Type:** display serif titles emerging from darkness; wide-tracked sans captions as museum labels; enormous whitespace.
- **Gold:** used as *light*, not fill — hairlines, the seal, hover glints, section dividers.
- **Imagery:** full-bleed, filmic, one subject per screen; macro craft interludes between narrative beats.
- **Commerce is the last room.** Price and cart stay quiet and subordinate until the customer has been moved; the store never greets — it *concludes*.

### 7.3 Emotional Experience
The scroll is a **descent through a private museum at night**: awe → reverence → the thrill of transformation → immersion in the collections → seeing oneself in the modern man → the quiet resolve to become a keeper. The site should feel **hushed, sovereign, warm and rare** — a house confident enough to withhold, to be still, and to let heritage do the selling. The final feeling at checkout is not *"I bought a shirt"* — it is ***"I became a keeper."***

### 7.4 Awwwards Inspiration (reference register, not to copy)
- **Cinematic scroll & heritage storytelling:** the reverent, film-like pacing of luxury-maison and fine-art-museum sites (Cartier / Van Cleef high-jewelry storytelling, Louis Vuitton *savoir-faire* chapters, Aesop's editorial restraint).
- **Craft-as-hero, macro-material sites** — where the product's making is the spectacle.
- **Slow, weighted, ink/gold transition work** and pinned-chapter scroll (the register of SOTD-winning narrative sites), applied with **more stillness and less spectacle** than a typical hype build.
- **The bar:** it should feel worthy of a *Site of the Day* not because it is busy, but because it is **restrained, cinematic and museum-grade** — the digital equivalent of walking into a dim gallery of India's stories, curated by someone who dresses like a king.

## APPENDIX — VISUAL LAWS (quick reference)

1. **India *and* luxury in every frame** — emerald + gold is the color of kings; use it as such.
2. **Neutral architecture, precious accents** — ~75% cream/ivory/charcoal, emerald/navy core, gold ≤10%, one collection accent.
3. **Gold is light, not paint** — foil, emboss, glint, thread; never flat gold ink.
4. **Two type voices, never crossed** — display serif for emotion, sans for information.
5. **Stillness is the luxury signal** — weighted slow motion, held frames, reverent camera.
6. **Prove the craft** — macro thread and puff; never assert luxury, show it.
7. **The seal is sacred** — the emblem is the Keeper's Mark; protect its clear space, variants and minimums.
8. **Nothing glossy, neon, or hype** — if it could be streetwear, it is wrong.

*NAVARN — the ancient, made new. Wear your heritage.*
